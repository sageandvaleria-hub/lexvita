About This Project

LexVita is a personal project — a space to share discoveries at the intersection of law and technology. It's not a law firm website. It's not a solicitation for clients. It's a hobbyist's notebook, made public.

Why?

The practice of law is changing. AI agents, automated research, digital case management, and new tools are reshaping how lawyers work. But most of these tools aren't built for lawyers — they're built for tech companies, and we have to adapt them.

This site is where I share what works, what doesn't, and what I'm learning along the way.

Who's Behind It?

A lawyer with a healthy obsession for using AI agents and automation to make legal practice more efficient. I run a small team of AI agents — MaiGai for legal research, Sewa for content creation, and Ushe for personal assistance — and I write about how to build and use these systems.

What You'll Find Here

  • Law discoveries — interesting cases, statutes, and legal principles I come across
  • Legal tech tips — how to use AI, automation, and digital tools in practice
  • Tool reviews — honest takes on software and services for lawyers
  • Practice notes — lessons from the trenches of legal practice

Not Legal Advice

Everything on this site is for informational and educational purposes only. Nothing here constitutes legal advice or creates an attorney-client relationship. If you need legal advice, consult a qualified lawyer licensed in your jurisdiction.
