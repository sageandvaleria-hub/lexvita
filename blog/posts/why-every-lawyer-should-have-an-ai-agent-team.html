Why Every Lawyer Should Have an AI Agent Team

26 June 2026 · AI Agents Legal Tech Productivity

I am a partner in a Lagos law firm. I also run a small team of AI agents. The difference? My AI agents don't take lunch breaks, don't ask for salary reviews, and don't complain about the heat in Lagos.

Let me introduce you to my team.

Meet the Agents

🦞 MaiGai — Legal Research Agent

MaiGai is my legal research assistant. He's specialized in maritime law, international trade, and petroleum downstream law — the areas I practice most. He searches through a reference library of over 60 documents (statutes, cases, treaties) using RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) to find relevant answers fast.

What he saves me: Hours of manual library research. Instead of flipping through PDFs, I ask MaiGai a question and get an answer with citations in seconds.

✍️ Sewa — Content Creator

Sewa handles content. She writes YouTube descriptions and blog posts for this very site. She's essentially a marketing department that fits in a terminal.

What she saves me: The mental energy of staring at a blank page. I give her bullet points, she gives me drafts. I edit. We publish.

📋 Ushe — Personal Assistant

Ushe is the air traffic controller. She manages my calendar, sets reminders, delegates tasks to MaiGai and Sewa, and sends me daily briefings. When a court date is coming up, Ushe makes sure I know about it — not just once, but with a three-tier notification system (3 days before, 1 day before, 2 hours before).

What she saves me: Missed deadlines, forgotten appointments, and the cognitive load of keeping everything in my head.

How They Work Together

Here's how it plays out in practice. I get a brief from a client. Ushe logs the deadline and schedules prep sessions. MaiGai researches the applicable law and flags any jurisdictional issues. Sewa drafts a client memo summarising the options. I review, tweak, and send. Total time: under an hour.

All of this happened without me writing a single line of code beyond the initial setup. The agents are built on skills — markdown files that define their capabilities, scripts they can run, and references they can consult.

You Don't Need to Be a Developer

The most common pushback I hear is "I'm not a programmer." Neither am I, really. These agents are configured with plain markdown files and simple Python scripts. The hardest part was deciding what I wanted them to do — the technical setup took an afternoon.

If you're a lawyer wondering whether AI can help your practice, the answer is yes. Start small. Pick one task you hate doing — research, drafting, scheduling — and build an agent for it. You'll wonder why you didn't do it sooner.
